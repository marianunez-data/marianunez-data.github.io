Problem

Reinforcing Compliance to Reduce Fines and Optimize Verification

Retail businesses selling age-restricted products face regulatory fines averaging $10,000 per violation when minors are not properly identified. Human verification is inconsistent, employees make errors under pressure during rush hours, shift changes, and high-volume periods.

A single non-compliance event can trigger license suspension and reputational damage far exceeding direct fine costs. Compliance burden scales linearly with headcount while accuracy remains unpredictably variable across staff, locations, and shifts.

Mission: Automate the first layer of age verification, ensuring no customer under 21 is ever auto-approved at point of sale.

Solution

A real‑time compliance layer from camera to decision in 17 ms

AgeGuard AI deploys a deep learning vision model at point of sale that predicts customer age from a single face image and triggers the appropriate compliance action, instantly, consistently, and at scale.

The three-tier alert system routes every transaction through one of three decision paths. No under-21 customer is ever auto-approved. Supervisors engage only for the ambiguous 21–25 window, eliminating unnecessary friction for the majority of adult customers.

RED — Predicted age < 21  ·  Block sale immediately
YELLOW — Predicted age 21–25  ·  Request physical ID
GREEN — Predicted age > 25  ·  Auto-approve, no friction
